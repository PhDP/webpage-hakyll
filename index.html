--- title: About ---

I'm a computational scientist working on machine learning, a branch of Artificial Intelligence. I'm working on inductive transfer, which in layman's terms could be described as the capacity to transfer knowledge from one task to another. It comes naturally to us, e.g.: we use what we learned for walking when learning to run, but machine learning algorithms build models from scratch. Not only is it inefficient, but being unable to rely on a vast and solid web of prior knowledge makes machine learning vulnerable to overfitting. I mostly use the rich (and complex!) data from ecological systems to develop and test my approaches.

I'm currently a Ph.D. candidate at the Theoretical Ecosystem Ecology laboratory and the Quebec Center for Biodiversity Science. My work is supported by an Alexander Graham Bell Graduate Scholarship from the NSERC. I'm a member of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers and their Computational Intelligence society.

I love programming! I was mostly programming in C and C++ but 2013 marks my transition to static functional languages (F#, Haskell, Scala).
